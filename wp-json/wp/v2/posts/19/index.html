Despite the beer world spending much of this year feeling like it\u2019s dealing in powder kegs rather than the ones we\u2019re more accustomed to such has been the minefield of obstacles to navigate, there will always be room, and indeed the need, to celebrate the wins.<\/strong><\/p>\n

So as the\u00a0Beer & Brewer<\/em>\u00a0Awards return for another year it\u2019s timelier than ever to be shining a spotlight on some of those who have raised the bar and given us all plenty of reasons to raise a toast.<\/p>\n

From a lifetime of achievement to a new brewing company that\u2019s spent the last 12 months high achieving, our winners highlight what is great and healthy about the broader industry at a time when it needs all the limelight it can get.<\/p>\n

Like last year, votes from our panel of judges (see below for the full list) were counted alongside ones from the public who gave their opinions via an online poll on our website back in September. It meant that the counting process was long and whilst some categories were largely a one-horse race, some were tightly contested which goes to show the level of competition, and passion, evident in the industry across the country.<\/p>\n

Congratulations to all our winners but the biggest toast must go to everyone in the industry who continue to strive for excellence and who celebrate our vibrant beer culture.<\/p>\n

These awards feature in the summer 2023\/24 edition of Beer & Brewer that is officially available from December 1. Head\u00a0<\/strong>here<\/strong>\u00a0to discover all of our great subscription offers.<\/strong><\/em><\/p>\n

The\u00a02023\u00a0Beer & Brewer\u00a0<\/em>Awards\u00a0winners<\/strong><\/h4>\n

Lifetime Achievement:<\/strong>
\nTina Panoutsos<\/strong>
\nFrom her support of the entire beer category, including from a sensory education and evaluation point of view as one of the world\u2019s best palates right through to her mentorship and care for a growing legion of women in the industry, Tina is held in the very highest regard and is the embodiment of what it means to win a lifetime achievement award.\u00a0Read our awards feature on Tina here.<\/strong><\/p>\n

Brewer of the Year: Matt Wilson, Moffat Beach Brewing<\/strong>
\nAfter repeat high achievement on the awards circuit for the past five years, Matt has steered Moffat Beach from very humble beginnings to the success story they now are, whilst keeping this family-run brewery\u2019s feet still very much firmly on the ground.
\nRead our awards feature on Matt here.<\/strong><\/p>\n

New Brewing Company of the Year: Du Cane Brewing<\/strong>
\nMuch like the mountain range they\u2019re named after, Launceston\u2019s Du Cane Brewing is a major tourist destination in a region renowned for them whilst concurrently being something that represents the local community right down to a tee.
\nRead our awards feature on Du Cane here.<\/strong><\/p>\n

Beer Venue of the Year:<\/strong>
\nThe Grain Store<\/strong>
\nIn their tenth year of operation, this Newcastle craft beer institution is the long overdue winner of this award and the first regional venue to do so. Always wholly and fiercely independent, The Grain Store is a spiritual home for Australian indie craft that backs it unconditionally at a time it needs all the backing it can get.\u00a0Read our awards feature on The Grain Store here.<\/strong><\/p>\n

Brewpub of the Year:<\/strong>
\nFelons Brewing Co<\/strong>
\nIn five years, Felons have gone from famously running out of beer in their opening few weeks to being an integral part of the cultural fabric of their home city. With an enviable and large location on Brisbane River, they are many things to many people, and are invariably busy. But their hospitality remains customer-focussed and their range of beer styles are always excellent.
